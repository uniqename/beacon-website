Module 03 — Trauma-informed case management from intake to closure
Module 03 — Case Management
BNB-DOC-03 Duration: 3 days / 24 hrs v1.0 · 2026
Facilitator notes: This 3-day curriculum is designed for new Case Managers who have a social work / counseling background but are new to DV-specific case management. Each session includes theory, skills practice, and reflection. Provide a 15-min break every 90 minutes. Never skip the wellbeing check-in at the start of each day.
Day 1 — Foundations of Trauma-Informed Care (8 hours)
TimeSessionContent / ActivitiesMaterials
9:00 – 9:30 Welcome & Wellbeing Check-in Ground rules. Introduce self-care protocol. Brief wellbeing round-table. Flip chart
9:30 – 11:00 Understanding Trauma What is trauma? How it affects the brain, body, and behavior.
Types of trauma (acute, complex, vicarious).
Activity: Case study — identify trauma responses in a fictional case. 		Slides, handout 1
11:00 – 11:15 Break
11:15 – 12:30 Trauma-Informed Principles The 6 SAMHSA principles: Safety, Trustworthiness, Peer support, Collaboration, Empowerment, Cultural humility.
Discussion: How do these apply on day 1 of a resident's arrival? 		Handout 2
12:30 – 1:30 Lunch
1:30 – 3:00 Survivor-Centered Practice What survivor-centered means (and doesn't mean).
Power dynamics in helping relationships.
Avoiding rescuer mentality.
Role play: First meeting with a new resident. 		Role play cards
3:00 – 3:15 Break
3:15 – 4:30 Trauma-Informed Language Language that helps vs. language that harms.
Avoiding victim-blaming phrases.
Cultural and religious sensitivity.
Activity: "What would you say?" — rewrite harmful scripts. 		Handout 3
4:30 – 5:00 Reflection & Close Key takeaways. Questions. Personal reflection journal (5 min). Preview Day 2. Journal prompt
Day 2 — The Beacon Case Management Cycle (8 hours)
TimeSessionContent / ActivitiesMaterials
9:00 – 9:15 Wellbeing Check-in How are staff feeling today? Brief check-in. Review Day 1 questions. —
9:15 – 10:45 Intake & Assessment Walk through BNB-02b (Resident Intake), BNB-03a (Survivor Assessment), and BNB-03b (Risk/Safety Assessment).
Practice: Pair role-play — one acts as Case Manager, one as a new resident. Complete forms in pairs. 		All three blank forms
10:45 – 11:00 Break
11:00 – 12:30 Safety Planning What is a safety plan? When to develop one (always!).
Components of a good safety plan: exit routes, code words, documents, contacts, phone, money.
The safety plan in the Beacon app vs. on paper.
Practice: Develop a sample safety plan for a fictional case. 		Safety plan template
12:30 – 1:30 Lunch
1:30 – 3:00 Care Planning & Goal Setting Walk through BNB-03c (90-Day Care Plan).
How to set goals WITH the survivor, not FOR them.
SMART goals in the context of trauma recovery.
Activity: Build a 90-day care plan for a fictional case. 		BNB-03c blank forms
3:00 – 3:15 Break
3:15 – 4:30 Progress Tracking & Reviews Walk through BNB-03d (Progress Tracking).
How to facilitate a 30/60/90-day review conversation.
When to escalate or revise the care plan.
Discussion: What do you do when a survivor isn't "progressing"? 		BNB-03d
4:30 – 5:00 Reflection & Close Key takeaways. Questions. Journal reflection. Preview Day 3. —
Day 3 — Referrals, Closures & Staff Wellbeing (8 hours)
TimeSessionContent / ActivitiesMaterials
9:00 – 9:15 Wellbeing Check-in Opening circle. How are staff feeling after 2 days of intensive learning? —
9:15 – 10:45 Referrals & Partnerships When to refer (and when NOT to).
Walk through BNB-04b (Legal Referral) and BNB-05a (MH Referral).
How to follow up on a referral.
Activity: Map your local referral network on a flip chart. 		Blank referral forms, local directory
10:45 – 11:00 Break
11:00 – 12:30 Case Closure & Follow-up Walk through BNB-02d (Discharge Plan) and BNB-03e (Case Closure).
What a "good" closure looks like — and the reality.
Tracking survivors after exit: legal, ethical, and safety considerations.
Discussion: How do you close a case when the survivor returns to the abuser? 		BNB-02d, BNB-03e
12:30 – 1:30 Lunch
1:30 – 3:00 Secondary Trauma & Staff Wellbeing What is secondary traumatic stress / vicarious trauma?
Signs you are experiencing it. Why it is not weakness.
Individual strategies. Organisational responsibilities.
Walk through BNB-05d (Staff Wellbeing Check-in form).
Activity: Personal wellbeing plan — 3 commitments. 		BNB-05d, handout 4
3:00 – 3:15 Break
3:15 – 4:30 Documentation, Records & Reporting Confidentiality — what goes in case notes, what doesn't.
How Beacon data feeds into impact reporting.
Common documentation errors and how to avoid them. 		Sample case notes
4:30 – 5:00 Certification & Close Post-training assessment (written, 10 questions). Certificate of completion. Group reflection: one word each. Assessment form, certificates
