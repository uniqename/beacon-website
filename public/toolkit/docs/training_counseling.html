Module 05 — Counseling & MH
BNB-DOC-05 For qualified counselors v1.0 · 2026
This curriculum is for use by qualified counselors or psychosocial support workers only. Case Managers without counseling qualifications should NOT deliver clinical sessions. They may co-facilitate psychoeducation sessions (Part 2) under qualified supervision.
Part 1 — Individual Counseling Framework

1.1 Theoretical Approach

Beacon's counseling program draws on trauma-focused Cognitive Behavioral Therapy (TF-CBT), narrative therapy approaches, and strengths-based practice. Sessions are non-directive and led by the survivor's readiness and goals.

1.2 Session Structure (Recommended)

StagePhaseFocusTypical Duration
Sessions 1–3StabilizationBuilding trust. Establishing safety. Grounding techniques. Psychoeducation on trauma responses. No trauma narrative yet.Weeks 1–3
Sessions 4–8ProcessingNarrative work when the survivor is ready. Cognitive restructuring (challenging self-blame). Grief and loss. Parenting impacts of DV. Strength identification.Weeks 2–6
Sessions 9–12IntegrationFuture orientation. Identity beyond survivorship. Relationship patterns. Practical life planning. Consolidating gains. Closure preparation.Weeks 4–12

1.3 Contraindications & Escalation

Immediate referral to psychiatry or hospital — Active suicidal ideation with plan and intent. Active psychosis. Substance intoxication in session.
Escalate to supervisor — Persistent dissociation in sessions. Trauma processing triggering overwhelming distress. Self-harm disclosures. Concern about capacity to consent.
Mandatory report — Disclosure of child abuse (current or ongoing). Risk to another identified person.
Part 2 — Group Psychoeducation Sessions (8-Session Series)

These group sessions are psychoeducational (not group therapy) and can be co-facilitated by a trained Case Manager under qualified supervision. Groups should be 6–10 participants maximum.

SessionTopicKey ContentDuration
1 Understanding What Happened to Me Types of abuse. Why abuse escalates. The cycle of violence. Identifying red flags. This is NOT your fault. 90 min
2 My Body and Trauma How trauma lives in the body. Common physical responses (hypervigilance, startle, fatigue). Simple grounding and breathing exercises. Normalizing physical symptoms. 90 min
3 Emotions and Healing The emotional rollercoaster of recovery. Naming emotions. Healthy vs. unhealthy coping. Anger is allowed. Grief is allowed. What to do when feelings are overwhelming. 90 min
4 Parenting Through Trauma How DV affects children. Supporting your children's recovery. Managing your own triggers as a parent. Protecting your children while protecting yourself. Children's resilience. 90 min
5 Safety — Then and Now Understanding safety planning. Identifying safe people. Recognising early warning signs in future relationships. Trusting your instincts again. 90 min
6 Relationships and Boundaries Healthy vs. unhealthy relationship patterns. What healthy relationships look like. Setting and holding boundaries. Recognising manipulation. When to ask for help. 90 min
7 Strengths and Identity Identifying personal strengths and survival skills. Your identity beyond this experience. Cultural and spiritual identity in healing. What you are building, not just recovering from. 90 min
8 Looking Forward Setting goals for the next chapter. Building a support network. What you need from people around you. Staying safe. The road ahead — 6-month, 1-year, 5-year vision. 90 min

Facilitation Notes for All Sessions:

Always open with a grounding exercise (2–3 mins breathing, feet on floor, name something you can see/hear).
Participation is always voluntary. Survivors can pass at any time. Never call on someone who hasn't volunteered to speak.
Close each session by naming something positive — a strength observed, a brave contribution, a moment of connection.
Have a private debrief available after sessions for any participant who is activated.
Record attendance (by Case #, not name) in BNB-05b (Group Session Log).
Part 3 — Peer Mentorship Program

Peer mentors are survivors who have completed the Beacon program and volunteer to support current residents. They provide hope, practical wisdom, and lived experience that staff cannot replicate.

Eligibility — Must have been out of shelter for at least 6 months. Must have stable housing and employment. Must have completed a peer mentor application (BNB-05c) and interview.
Training — All peer mentors receive a 1-day orientation covering: role boundaries, confidentiality, mandatory reporting, what to do in a crisis, and self-care. Refresher training every 6 months.
Role — Peer mentors do NOT provide case management or counseling. They share their story (what they choose), accompany residents to appointments (with staff coordination), participate in group sessions as invited guests, and meet one-to-one with a matched resident (supervised).
Support for peer mentors — Monthly group peer mentor meeting. Access to counselor for their own support (free, as part of Beacon commitment). Named supervisor for any concerns. Right to step back without stigma.
Part 4 — Staff Wellbeing Protocol

Working with survivors of trauma is psychologically demanding. Beacon's commitment to staff wellbeing is non-negotiable — staff who are not supported cannot support others.

Monthly wellbeing check-in — Every Case Manager and counselor completes BNB-05d monthly. The Program Coordinator reviews and responds within 5 working days.
Reflective supervision — Every Case Manager receives at least 1 hour of reflective supervision per month with their supervisor. This is separate from line management and focuses on emotional and professional wellbeing.
Peer support — Weekly team check-in (15 mins, not a case review). A "parking lot" list of things to worry about together so they don't fester individually.
Right to debrief — After any critical incident, every staff member involved has the right to a same-day debrief. This is mandatory, not optional, for the organisation — never optional for the individual.
External counseling — Beacon will provide access to at least free counseling sessions per year for any staff member. This is confidential and not shared with management.
