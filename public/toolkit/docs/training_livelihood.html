Module 06 — Livelihood
BNB-DOC-06 Duration: 8 weeks v1.0 · 2026
How to use this curriculum: Adapt based on the skills assessment results (BNB-06a). Run parallel tracks — not all survivors follow the same path. Weeks 1–3 are foundation weeks everyone attends. From Week 4, split into tracks based on interest and skills. Always deliver with a trauma-informed approach — at the survivor's pace, celebrating small wins.

Weeks 4–8 — Skill Tracks (Run Simultaneously)

After completing the skills assessment (BNB-06a), each survivor is matched to one primary track. More than one track can run at the same time if facilitated by different coordinators or volunteers.

Facilitation Guidelines
Trauma awareness — Some survivors may feel shame about gaps in their education or work history. Never ask survivors to share their level of education or previous income publicly. All skills work is framed around what they CAN do.
Practical over theoretical — Every session should have a practical activity. Survivors learn by doing. Minimize lecturing.
Childcare — If childcare is not provided, workshops must be scheduled around school hours. Never put a survivor in a position where they must choose between the program and their children.
Collect feedback every session — Use BNB-06c (Workshop Feedback). Review results before the next session and adjust.
Celebrate progress — Recognize milestones: first CV complete, first job application sent, first mock interview passed. Small acknowledgements matter enormously to survivors who have been told they are worthless.
