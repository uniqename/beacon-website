This toolkit enables organizations in Ghana, the United States, and beyond to replicate Beacon's integrated survivor support model — combining shelter, counseling, legal advocacy, and livelihood empowerment — within 3–4 months, without starting from scratch. It is a living document, updated as Beacon learns from each new site.

90-Day Launch Timeline
Month 1
Assess & Register
  • Complete site readiness checklist
  • File legal entity registration
  • Identify shelter location
  • Hire case manager lead
  • Sign Beacon partner MOU
Month 2
Build & Train
  • Complete staff trauma training
  • Adapt intake protocols locally
  • Set up Beacon app (local config)
  • Execute legal & health MOUs
  • Run community sensitization
Month 3
Soft Launch
  • Accept first 3–5 residents
  • Begin case management cycles
  • Launch livelihood workshops
  • Activate app referral network
  • Submit Month 1 data to Beacon HQ
Month 4
Full Operations
  • Reach full shelter capacity
  • Peer mentorship program active
  • Employer partnerships signed
  • Board advisory group formed
  • Publish local impact baseline

Site Readiness Checklist
Complete before applying to become a Beacon partner site.
  • Dedicated staff lead identified (min. 1 FTE)
  • Physical location available or secured
  • Community need documented (data or assessment)
  • Local legal/healthcare referral partner identified
  • Organization legally registered (or in process)
  • Minimum 6-month operating budget secured
  • Leadership committed to trauma-informed principles
  • Data protection & survivor privacy plan in place
  • Community trust-building plan for target population
  • Cultural adaptation plan for local context
Partner Site Eligibility Criteria
  • Shares Beacon's survivor-centered, trauma-informed values
  • Leadership committed to non-discrimination policy
  • Willing to collect & share anonymized outcome data
  • Commits to Beacon branding & quality standards
  • Has existing community relationships in target area
  • At least 1 staff member completes Beacon training
  • Signs Beacon Partner MOU (annual renewal)
  • Participates in quarterly partner network calls
Monitoring & Evaluation Framework
Survivor Outcomes
  • % achieving stable housing post-exit
  • % with income source at 6 months
  • % who complete legal process (if applicable)
  • Wellbeing score change (intake vs. exit)
  • % who return to abusive situation
  • Children in school at 6-month follow-up
Program Performance
  • Shelter occupancy rate (target: 80%+)
  • Average length of stay (target: 60–90 days)
  • Case management sessions per resident/month
  • Partner referrals completed on time
  • Staff turnover rate (target: <20%/yr)
  • App active users & engagement rate
Reporting to Beacon HQ
  • Monthly: intake/exit numbers, incidents
  • Quarterly: outcome data, financials, staffing
  • Annually: full impact report (using template)
  • All data anonymized — no survivor names shared
  • Submitted via Beacon admin dashboard
  • Beacon HQ provides aggregate network report
