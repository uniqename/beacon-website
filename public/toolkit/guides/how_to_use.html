Purpose of this guide: This document explains every section of the Beacon NGO Replication Toolkit — what each module covers, what each form is for, when to use it, and how to fill it out correctly. Read this first before opening any forms. Return to it whenever you are unsure about a document.
How the Toolkit is Organized

The toolkit is organized into 8 modules, each covering a core program area. Within each module you will find:

  • Forms — Fillable documents used with individual survivors or in daily operations. Fill these out per person or per incident.
  • Templates — Documents you customize once and reuse (e.g. MOUs, agreements, budget). Edit the text in brackets with your organization's details.
  • Guides — Reference documents and information sheets you distribute or consult.
Adding new forms: Copy any existing HTML file. Open it in a text editor (TextEdit, Notepad, VS Code). Change the title, form number, and fields. Save with a new filename. Then add a link in index.html. The shared stylesheet handles all formatting automatically.
Printing: Every form is print-ready. Press Cmd+P (Mac) or Ctrl+P (Windows) → Save as PDF, or print directly. Input fields show as clean lines when printed.
Module 01 — Organizational Setup

Use these before you open your doors. They establish your legal foundation, governance, and formal relationship with Beacon HQ.

📋 Site Readiness Assessment · forms/site_readiness.html

What it is: A checklist that confirms your organization has the minimum conditions to become a Beacon partner site.

When to use: Complete this before applying to Beacon. If you score below 80%, address gaps before submitting.

How to fill out:

  1. Work through each section honestly. Tick only what is fully in place.
  2. For anything unticked, note the gap in the "Action needed" column.
  3. Calculate your readiness score at the bottom.
  4. Attach your legal registration documents, budget summary, and staff list when submitting to Beacon HQ.
📋 Partner Application · forms/partner_application.html

What it is: The formal application to become a Beacon Affiliate, Partner Site, or Hub Site.

When to use: After completing the Site Readiness Assessment with a passing score.

How to fill out:

  1. Fill out all organizational details in Section 1.
  2. Describe your target population and community context in Section 2 — be specific about the DV/homelessness landscape in your area.
  3. In Section 3, select the support tier you are applying for and explain why.
  4. Have your Executive Director sign Section 4.
  5. Email the completed PDF to ename@beaconnewbeginnings.org with subject: "Partner Application — [Your Org Name]".
📄 Partner MOU · templates/partner_mou.html

What it is: A Memorandum of Understanding between your organization and Beacon HQ that formalizes the partnership.

How to customize: Replace all [BRACKETED] text with your organization's actual details. Have a lawyer review before signing.

Module 02 — Shelter Operations

These are your daily operational forms. Some are filled out once per resident; others are used daily or per incident.

📋 Shelter Setup Checklist · forms/shelter_checklist.html

What it is: A one-time checklist to verify your physical shelter meets minimum safety and operational standards before accepting residents.

How to fill out: Walk through the physical space with your site manager. Tick each item only when it is fully confirmed. Document the date each item was completed. Keep a signed copy on file and send one to Beacon HQ before opening.

📋 Resident Intake Form · forms/resident_intake.html

What it is: The first form completed when a survivor arrives at the shelter. Captures demographics, immediate needs, safety concerns, and consent.

When to use: Complete within the first 2 hours of arrival — but only after the survivor has had water, food, and a moment to settle.

How to fill out:

  1. Use a calm, private space. Have a female staff member present for female survivors.
  2. Ask questions conversationally — do not read them mechanically from the form.
  3. Section 1 (Demographics): fill with the survivor's help, but don't push for information they're not ready to give. Mark "declined" rather than leaving blank.
  4. Section 2 (Immediate Needs): tick all that apply — this triggers what you provide in the first 24 hours.
  5. Section 3 (Safety): ask gently. Does the perpetrator know this location? Do they have weapons? This informs immediate safety steps.
  6. Section 4 (Children): document every child present — this is legally important.
  7. Section 5 (Consent): read the consent statement aloud and have the survivor sign or mark their acknowledgement. Never skip this.
  8. File the original securely. Never leave this form visible on a desk or screen.
Confidentiality: This form must be stored in a locked cabinet or password-protected file. Survivor names must NEVER appear in any external reporting.
📋 Incident Report · forms/incident_report.html

What it is: Documents any safety incident, policy violation, or unusual event at the shelter.

When to use: Any time there is a physical altercation, self-harm concern, unauthorized visitor, fire, theft, or staff misconduct allegation.

How to fill out: Complete within 2 hours of the incident. Be factual — describe what you observed, not what you inferred. Have a witness sign. Submit to the site manager and send a copy to Beacon HQ within 24 hours.

📋 Discharge & Transition Plan · forms/discharge_plan.html

What it is: A collaborative plan created with the survivor when they are ready to leave the shelter.

How to fill out: Complete this together with the survivor — not for them. Document their exit destination, referrals made, safety plan, and 6-month follow-up schedule. The survivor keeps a copy; you keep one on file.

Module 03 — Case Management

These forms are the backbone of your service. Each survivor should have a complete case file with all five documents.

📋 Survivor Intake Assessment · forms/survivor_assessment.html

What it is: A deeper assessment completed 48–72 hours after arrival (once the survivor is more settled). Captures history, goals, strengths, and trauma background to inform the care plan.

How to fill out:

  1. This is a conversation, not an interrogation. Use open-ended questions.
  2. Do not push for details about the abuse. Follow the survivor's lead.
  3. Section on strengths is critical — always identify what the person does well and what support they have.
  4. The goals section should be in the survivor's own words wherever possible.
  5. Case manager completes their notes section after the conversation, not during.
📋 Risk & Safety Assessment · forms/risk_safety_assessment.html

What it is: A structured lethality and safety assessment used to determine the level of danger and inform the safety plan.

When to use: At intake and whenever there is a change in the survivor's situation (perpetrator released, new threats, etc.).

How to fill out: Answer each question based on what the survivor tells you. High-risk factors should immediately trigger escalation to your legal partner and a safety planning session. Document the resulting safety plan on the last page.

High-risk indicators: Weapons access, strangulation history, threats to kill, escalating frequency, and perpetrator stalking are the highest lethality predictors. Any one of these = immediate safety planning and possible law enforcement contact.
📋 90-Day Individual Care Plan · forms/care_plan_90day.html

What it is: The core working document for each survivor's journey — covering housing, legal, mental health, livelihood, and children's goals over a 90-day cycle.

How to fill out:

  1. Complete this together with the survivor at the end of Week 1, after the Intake Assessment and Risk Assessment are done.
  2. For each goal area, write 1–3 specific, achievable goals. Use the survivor's language.
  3. Assign each goal a responsible party (survivor, case manager, legal partner, etc.) and a target date.
  4. Review and update every 30 days. Document what changed in the review notes column.
  5. At day 90, use outcomes from this plan to complete the Progress Tracking form and decide on renewal or closure.
📋 Progress Tracking Form · forms/progress_tracking.html

What it is: A bi-weekly or monthly check-in that documents progress against the care plan.

How to fill out: For each care plan goal, rate progress on the 1–5 scale. Add brief narrative notes on what moved forward, what stalled, and why. Use this data to complete your monthly report to Beacon HQ.

📋 Case Closure Form · forms/case_closure.html

What it is: Documents the outcome when a survivor's case formally closes — whether through positive exit, self-discharge, or other reasons.

How to fill out: Complete the reason for closure, exit destination, referrals made, and outcome ratings honestly. Even difficult closures (self-discharge against advice) should be documented without judgment. Schedule the 30-day and 6-month follow-up check-ins before closing the file.

Module 04 — Legal Advocacy
📋 Evidence Documentation Log · forms/evidence_log.html

What it is: A log for documenting and preserving evidence of abuse (photos, medical records, messages, witness statements) in a way that may be used in legal proceedings.

How to fill out: Each row = one piece of evidence. Record the date obtained, type, description, how it was preserved (digital copy, original on file), and chain of custody. Never alter evidence. Your legal partner should review this log before any court proceedings.

Important: Advise survivors not to delete anything from their devices. Even deleted messages may be recoverable. Contact your legal partner immediately if there is an upcoming court date.
📋 Legal Referral Form · forms/legal_referral.html

What it is: A referral document sent to your legal aid partner when a survivor needs legal support.

How to fill out: Fill in Section 1 (survivor details — use a case number, not full name, in external documents). Section 2 describes the legal need (protection order, divorce, custody, criminal case). Section 3 documents urgency. Fax or hand-deliver; do not email survivor details without encryption.

Module 05 — Counseling & Mental Health
📋 Mental Health Referral · forms/mh_referral.html

What it is: A referral form sent to your healthcare/counseling partner when a survivor needs mental health services beyond what your case manager can provide.

How to fill out: Describe the presenting concern in clinical but plain language. Document consent. Include urgency level. Follow up within 5 business days to confirm the appointment was made.

📋 Group Session Log · forms/group_session_log.html

What it is: A session record for group therapy, peer support, or workshop sessions.

How to fill out: Record attendance by case number only (no full names in group documents). Document the session topic, facilitator notes, and any concerns to follow up individually. File after each session.

📋 Staff Wellbeing Check-in · forms/staff_wellbeing.html

What it is: A monthly self-assessment for staff and volunteers working with trauma survivors.

How to fill out: Staff complete this privately and share results with the site manager if they choose. If any staff member scores in the "high burnout" zone, a supervised debrief should be scheduled within one week. This is not a performance review — it is a care tool.

Modules 06–08 — Livelihood, Technology & Fundraising
📋 Skills Assessment · forms/skills_assessment.html

What it is: Identifies a survivor's existing skills, interests, and work history to match them with livelihood programs and job opportunities.

How to fill out: Complete this together with the survivor around Week 2–3, once they are settled. Focus on what they enjoy and have done — not on what they lack. Use the results to enroll them in the right workshop track and flag potential employer matches.

📋 Job Placement Tracker · forms/job_placement_tracker.html

What it is: A running log of job applications, interviews, placements, and outcomes for all survivors in the livelihood program.

How to fill out: Update in real time. Each row = one application or opportunity. Track outcomes (hired, rejected, pending). This data feeds directly into your impact report and Beacon HQ quarterly submission.

📋 Digital Safety Assessment · forms/digital_safety_assessment.html

What it is: Assesses whether a survivor's devices and online accounts may be monitored by the perpetrator.

How to fill out: Complete at intake with the Resident Intake Form. Walk through each question with the survivor. If any monitoring concern is identified, follow the device safety protocol in the app setup guide before giving the survivor access to Beacon's digital tools.

📋 Grant Tracker & Donor Log · forms/grant_tracker.html · forms/donor_log.html

Grant Tracker: Logs every grant application — funder name, amount requested, deadline, status, and outcome. Update weekly. Use this to manage your funding pipeline and avoid missed deadlines.

Donor Log: Tracks individual and corporate donors — contact info, giving history, last communication, and follow-up dates. The foundation of your donor stewardship program.

Customizing & Expanding the Toolkit

To edit a form:

  1. Open the HTML file in any text editor
  2. Find and replace [BRACKETED] text with your org's details
  3. Add or remove <div class="field-group"> blocks to change fields
  4. Save the file — changes apply immediately

To add a new form:

  1. Copy any existing form file
  2. Rename it (e.g. forms/my_new_form.html)
  3. Update the title, module badge, and fields
  4. Add a link in index.html under the right module
