Module 07 — Setup, configuration, and staff training for the Beacon digital platform
Module 07 — Digital & App
BNB-GUIDE-07 v1.0 · 2026
Read before going live with the app at your site. Complete the Digital Safety Assessment (BNB-07a) with every survivor before giving them access to the app or any digital Beacon tools. If in doubt about any survivor's digital safety situation, use the shelter device only.
Part 1 — Site Setup & Configuration
Step 1 — Contact Beacon HQ for access
Email ename@beaconnewbeginnings.org to request site admin credentials. You will receive a Site Admin account linked to your Beacon partner agreement. Setup takes 2–3 business days.
Step 2 — Configure site profile
Log into the admin dashboard. Set your site name, city, and region. Upload your site logo (optional). Add your local emergency numbers — these appear in the survivor's app emergency screen. Critical: verify these numbers work before going live.
Step 3 — Add local hotline numbers
The app's emergency screen shows a list of local helplines. Go to Settings → Local Contacts and add:

Step 4 — Set language preferences
Go to Settings → Languages. Enable all languages spoken by your survivor population. Current available languages: English, Twi, Hausa, French. Request additional languages from Beacon HQ if needed.
Step 5 — Create staff accounts
Go to Admin → Staff Accounts. Add each staff member with role-based access:
RoleAccess Level
Site Admin (Program Coordinator)Full access — all cases, reports, settings
Case ManagerTheir assigned cases only + referral tools
CounselorMental health notes + session logs only
Livelihood CoordinatorLivelihood module + job placement tracker
Step 6 — Test before going live
Create a test case. Run through the full intake flow. Test the emergency button. Verify that hotline numbers connect. Complete a test Digital Safety Assessment. Only go live once all tests pass.
Part 2 — Staff Training on the Admin Dashboard

All Case Managers must complete this orientation before using the platform for any live cases. Estimated time: 2 hours.

Logging in & passwords — Never share login credentials with another staff member. If you suspect your account has been accessed by someone else, change your password immediately and notify the Site Admin. Use a strong password (12+ characters, not reused from other accounts).
Opening a new case — Go to Cases → New Case. Enter the Case # (not the survivor's full name). Select their assigned Case Manager. Complete the intake checklist. Attach all completed forms (upload PDFs or fill the digital versions).
Adding case notes — Case notes must be entered within 24 hours of any interaction. Use objective language ("Survivor reported feeling unsafe at work. Referral to legal partner initiated."). Never include personal opinions or information that could identify a third party.
Referral tracking — All referrals made (legal, counseling, livelihood, medical) must be logged under the case. Include the referral date, partner name, and whether the appointment was attended. Follow up within 5 days if there is no update.
Evidence tool — The evidence tool stores encrypted, time-stamped files. Walk the survivor through using it only after completing the Digital Safety Assessment (BNB-07a). Explain what will happen to the evidence (stays encrypted in their account, shareable only by them with legal partner).
Reporting — At the end of each month, go to Reports → Monthly Summary. Review your caseload stats. Submit the quarterly data report to Beacon HQ via Admin → Reporting → Submit.
Part 3 — The Survivor-Facing App

The Beacon app is designed for survivors to use independently on their own (safe) devices. Do NOT set up the app on a shared or unsafe device.

Stealth mode — The app can be disguised as a utilities app (calculator, weather, health tracker). Activate this under App Settings → Disguise Mode. Show the survivor how to activate and deactivate it. Explain that if someone sees the app and asks, the disguised version shows normal content.
Quick exit button — There is a visible "X" button that immediately closes the app and clears recent activity. Show the survivor where it is and practice pressing it. It should become reflex. The "Quick Exit" feature also allows the survivor to set a password that opens the disguised version so the real content stays hidden.
Emergency button — The emergency button sends a pre-configured distress signal to the survivor's nominated safe contact AND shows emergency numbers. Walk the survivor through setting up their emergency contact (must be someone who knows they are in the shelter, and who is safe to contact).
Journaling and evidence — Survivors can journal privately and store evidence (photos, screenshots, recordings). Everything is encrypted. The survivor is the only one who can share it — not staff.
Resources — The app contains local legal rights guides (from Module 04), safety planning checklists, and livelihood resources. Customise for your site in Admin → Resources.
When NOT to set up the app — If the Digital Safety Assessment (BNB-07a) shows high monitoring risk and the survivor's device may be compromised, do NOT set up the app on their device. Use a shelter device instead, or defer until digital safety is established.
Part 4 — Data Privacy & Security Policy
Data minimisation — Only collect data you need. Do not enter personal information (full name, national ID, address) into any digital system unless required for a specific legal or referral purpose.
Data is never sold — Beacon HQ does not sell, rent, or share survivor data with any commercial entity. Aggregate, anonymised data may be used for impact reporting with funder permission.
Data retention — Case records are retained for after case closure in line with local regulations. After this period, all personally identifiable data is deleted. Impact statistics are kept permanently in aggregate form.
Breach protocol — If you suspect a data breach (unauthorised access to case records, a lost device with case data, a compromised account), notify the Site Admin and Beacon HQ immediately. Do not wait. The Site Admin will follow the breach response protocol and notify affected individuals within 72 hours as required by law.
Survivor's right to their data — A survivor may request to see their case records at any time. They may also request deletion of their data after exit. Work with Beacon HQ to process this request within 30 days.
