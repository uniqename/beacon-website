Job Description 1 — Case Manager

About the Role

The Case Manager is at the heart of Beacon's work. You will be the primary support person for 5–10 survivors at any time, guiding them through safety planning, legal referrals, mental health support, and livelihood planning during their time at the shelter. You will use trauma-informed, survivor-centred approaches in all your work.

Key Responsibilities

Qualifications & Experience

Personal Attributes

Empathetic, non-judgmental, and deeply committed to survivor agency. Able to stay calm in a crisis. Committed to confidentiality. Willing to work flexible hours when needed. Shares Beacon's values of dignity, safety, and empowerment.

Job Description 2 — Program Coordinator / Site Lead

About the Role

The Program Coordinator is responsible for the day-to-day operations of the Beacon site. You will supervise the case management team, manage partnerships, oversee data and reporting, and ensure the program is delivered with fidelity to the Beacon model. This role requires both operational leadership and hands-on program involvement.

Job Description 3 — Livelihood & Vocational Coordinator

About the Role

The Livelihood Coordinator designs and delivers skills and job-readiness workshops, manages employer partnerships, and supports survivors to achieve economic independence. You will manage the job placement tracker and follow up with employed graduates for 6 months post-placement.
