⚠ SAFETY-CRITICAL — Must be complete before first resident arrives
All entry doors have working locks. Keys held only by authorized staff.
Windows have functional latches or security bars where appropriate.
Shelter address is known only to staff and trusted referral partners — not publicly listed.
A functional alarm or alert system is in place (even basic: a locked gate + phone contact protocol).
Emergency exit routes are identified, unobstructed, and known to all staff.
First aid kit is stocked and accessible to staff (not locked away).
Emergency contact numbers are posted visibly (police, hospital, legal partner, Beacon HQ).
A crisis de-escalation protocol document is accessible to all staff.
A. Physical Space
Sleeping areas have beds, mattresses, and basic bedding for each resident.
Functional bathrooms/toilets with running water and sanitation supplies.
A shared kitchen or cooking area is equipped and functional.
A private space for case management meetings is available (can be a curtained area).
A space for group activities or sessions is available.
Lighting is adequate in all areas, including at night.
Children's sleeping space and play area are identified (if shelter admits families).
B. Supplies & Operations
Hygiene starter kits are available for new residents (soap, toothbrush, etc.).
Basic food supplies or food sourcing plan is in place for Week 1.
Locked filing cabinet (or password-protected digital system) for resident files.
Phone or communication device available for resident emergency use.
House rules document is printed and posted visibly.
Resident rights charter is printed and available in local language(s).
A daily operations schedule template is prepared for staff.
C. Staff & Protocols
At least 1 trained staff member available on-site during all hours residents are present.
Staff have completed trauma-informed care orientation (Beacon training or equivalent).
Incident Report form is printed and accessible to all staff.
Intake forms are printed / digital and ready to use.
Staff have been briefed on confidentiality policy — no photos, no names shared externally.
A volunteer onboarding procedure is in place (if using volunteers).
D. Community & Partners
Nearest hospital / health clinic location and hours are documented.
Legal aid partner contact is confirmed and responsive.
Nearest police station and DV unit (if applicable) contact is documented.
At least 1 referral pathway for residents who need additional services is mapped out.
