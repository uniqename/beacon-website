OUR RESPONSE TO YOUR PROPOSED TERMS

1. Structured Referral Process with Designated Contacts

We are fully in agreement. We have already developed a detailed Referral Pathway Document which establishes step-by-step referral procedures, designated liaison contacts, referral form standards, and 30/90-day follow-up protocols. We are happy to share this document for your review and to adapt it based on your team's input.

2. Definition of Client Categories

We welcome and support this structure. We propose the following three-tier classification for BNB-referred clients, aligned with your suggested categories:

  • Fully Paying Clients — individuals referred by BNB who have the financial capacity to cover the full cost of services. BNB will confirm this at the point of referral.
  • Partially Supported Clients — individuals for whom BNB will co-fund a portion of service costs through our donor-funded programs, with the client contributing based on assessed capacity.
  • Exceptional Cases — a limited number of individuals per quarter presenting with acute need and no financial capacity, referred by BNB with full documentation. These will be handled case by case, as you have indicated, and subject to prior agreement.

We are open to agreeing specific quotas and criteria for each category during our follow-up meeting.

3. Agreement on Payment Responsibilities and Timelines

We confirm our commitment to ensuring that payment responsibilities are clearly established before any referral results in service delivery. BNB will include a confirmed payment category on every referral form submitted to your team. For partially supported cases, BNB will issue a written payment commitment letter at the time of referral.

4. Pilot Phase

We strongly support the introduction of a pilot phase. We propose a structured 90-day pilot beginning from the date of MOU signing, with the following parameters:

  • A defined maximum number of referrals during the pilot period (we suggest 5–10 cases) to allow both teams to assess operational alignment
  • At least one client from each of the three payment categories, where possible
  • A mid-pilot check-in at 45 days between both liaisons
  • A formal pilot review at 90 days to assess outcomes, surface challenges, and agree on the full partnership framework going forward

We have prepared a Pilot Phase Framework document which we enclose with this letter for your review.

5. Name Correction

Thank you for the important clarification. We confirm that all future correspondence, documentation, and agreements will reflect the correct name: The Brain and Sleep Medical Centre Ltd. We will update our existing documents accordingly.

PROPOSED NEXT STEPS

  1. Your team reviews the enclosed documents: Pilot Phase Framework, Draft MOU, and Referral Pathway
  2. We schedule a meeting at your convenience to discuss terms, finalize client categories and payment arrangements, and agree on pilot parameters
  3. Both parties sign the MOU and Pilot Phase Framework to formally launch the partnership
  4. Pilot phase begins within two weeks of signing
