About Beacon of New Beginnings

BNB is a Ghana-based nonprofit serving vulnerable individuals through a range of integrated support services:

  • Case Management — structured, individualized care plans
  • Counselling Referrals — connecting clients to professional psychological support
  • Skills Training — vocational and life skills programs
  • Housing Stability — housing navigation and support
  • Legal Resource Navigation — access to legal information and resources
  • Community Reintegration — rebuilding social confidence and belonging

Why Partner with PsyPro Ghana?

Many of our clients carry unaddressed psychological burdens — trauma, anxiety, depression, and grief — that significantly impede their progress toward stability and independence. Professional psychological assessment and therapy is a critical bridge in their recovery.

PsyPro Ghana's expertise in psychological services and professional development makes it an ideal referral partner. We envision a relationship where:

  • BNB clients can access timely, professional psychological assessment and therapy through PsyPro Ghana
  • PsyPro Ghana can refer clients who need social stabilization, case management, or community support back to BNB
  • Our organizations coordinate care in a structured, client-centered way

Proposed Partnership Terms

1. Referral Pathway

  • BNB will identify clients in need of psychological support and issue a formal referral to PsyPro Ghana
  • PsyPro Ghana will conduct intake assessments and provide appropriate psychological services
  • With client consent, brief case updates will be shared between teams to ensure holistic care

2. Fee Structure Considerations

We understand that PsyPro Ghana operates on a full-payment model for new clients, with discounts extended to returning clients. We respect this structure and propose:

  • Exploring a designated allocation of returning-client discount rates for BNB-referred individuals who have received prior services
  • Working with BNB's donor funding to cover or subsidize client costs for the most financially vulnerable referrals
  • A collaborative conversation around any future possibilities for sliding-scale access as the partnership matures

3. MOU and Ongoing Collaboration

We propose a Memorandum of Understanding to formalize this relationship, covering referral protocols, confidentiality, communication standards, and review timelines.

Next Steps

As indicated in our conversation, you would like to review our formal proposal before moving forward. We suggest:

  • Scheduling a follow-up discussion to finalize terms
  • Drafting and reviewing a Memorandum of Understanding
